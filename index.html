The Foundation

A Framework Born From Scripture

The Lamplit 12-Type Framework wasn't adapted from secular categories. It was built by studying Scripture itself.

We observed how different personalities have always expressed faith—from the builders and prophets of the Old Testament to the teachers and companions of the early church. What emerged was a pattern visible across the biblical narrative: a pattern that illuminates how God, in His creativity, designed different people to know Him in different ways.

You're not an anomaly. You're part of a pattern as old as faith itself.

Orientation

The direction of your spiritual heart

Devoted

Your spiritual center is your personal relationship with God. You're drawn to worship, prayer, and direct communion with the divine.

"Love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your mind." — Matthew 22:37

Connected

Your spiritual center is your relationships with others. You experience God most vividly through community, service, and love in action.

"Love your neighbor as yourself." — Matthew 22:39

Becoming

Your spiritual center is transformation itself. You're drawn to growth, learning, and the ongoing process of sanctification.

"Be transformed by the renewing of your mind." — Romans 12:2

Expression

How your faith takes form

Active

You express faith through doing. Building, creating, organizing—your spirituality takes tangible form.

"Faith without works is dead." — James 2:26

Contemplative

You express faith through reflection. Study, meditation, discernment—you process truth deeply before acting.

"In quietness and trust is your strength." — Isaiah 30:15

Relational

You express faith through connection. Encouraging, nurturing, walking alongside—your spirituality is inherently interpersonal.

"By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you love one another." — John 13:35

Protective

You express faith through advocacy. Speaking truth, defending the vulnerable—you cannot stay silent when something matters.

"Speak up for those who cannot speak for themselves." — Proverbs 31:8

3 Orientations × 4 Expressions = 12 Types

These dimensions intersect to reveal twelve distinct spiritual personality types—twelve ways of walking with God that have been visible in His people from Genesis to Revelation. Each type connects to a biblical figure who embodies that personality's expression of faith.
